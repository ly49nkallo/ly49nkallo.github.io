My Homepage is a Love Letter to Programming

July 11, 2026

Today, OpenAI released GPT 5.6 Sol. It is smarter than me and better at programming than me.

It has gotten to the point that it would not just be unthinkably ineffiencent, but in fact unethical for me to write code by hand anymore.

Then I became really sad.

At one point, I would have considered myself the best programmer I knew. I have made around 100 repositories on Github (both public and private) due to the sheer volume of random passion projects I would undertake. I recreated PyTorch from scratch when I was in 10th grade. I designed and mostly implemented a fully custom programming language named after myself that had no objects, loops, or flow control. The same day I learned about 2D fourier transforms, I wrote one in Java (with libGDX) with custom complex math while sitting in my Mom's office. If you asked my about my opinion on certain coding practices (or C++ in general), I would likely talk non-stop for 2 hours, including all the little quirks and caveats about the most minute details of how these languages worked. Somewhere on this homepage will be an exhaustive list of everything I have done (WIP).

I am completely fluent in Vim keybinds, if that earns any respect anymore.

Honestly, the coding LLMs have been better than me for a long time now--it was only a matter of adoption. I vibe-code a lot now. I have a Codex subscription and a Windsurf subscriptiion that I pay for out-of-pocket. All of my smartest friends use LLMs to code and are very open about discussing the state of the art vice-coding techniques. I have had many extended conversations about vibecoding, and they always feel like the fall of Rome.

The problem with those conversations was that they were futile. Vibecoding is objectively much easier than writing code by hand, and the code that it produces is much safer, more correct, less buggy, more maintainable, and more tested than anything even the most senior programmer could concieve--all in a laughable fraction of the time.

It will never go away, and with it's ever expanding dominance over coding, I have never been more certain. It would be unethical to ban it (besides being just plain ineffiencent) and it has obvious upsides. Even if every major supplier of LLMs were legally required to remove their service this very instant, open weight models like GLM are mere months behind in progress and are more than suited to fill in their now empty shoes.

What I Have Decided to Do About It

Its been several months now, and it feels as through I have lost a part of my identity. Writing code by hand has become unthinkable, both for personal projects and professional applications. Within the year, I was writing every single line myself, spending hours and hours print debugging silly errors and typos in function signatures. I was writing a Chess Engine and Bot in C (and not Python) purely for the love of the game. Now that part of me has died completely.

I remembered making my own webpages in middle and highschool. I have always wanted a homepage for my own to play with (and to reminiss about an era before FaceBook and other forms of Social media which killed homepages). That is why I have decided to make this webpage today.

The above reason outlines clearly why this website is written in plain, static HTML+CSS. I could spend time beatifying it, but the uglyness is the point--websites that look like this will never be made again.

This is my final push against the AI.

The final monument to my own technical expertise I have spent so long cultivating.

A reason to open VSCode (by beloved) and not just the Codex CLI.

And finally, a place to express my own creativity by sharing my personal projects--what could be more beautiful way to commemorate the death of coding?
